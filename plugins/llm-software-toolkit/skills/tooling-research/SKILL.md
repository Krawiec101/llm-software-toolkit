# Tooling Research

Read `references/php-api-skill-contract.md`; this skill researches only and never installs findings.

1. Define the decision, current solution and compatibility constraints. Search in order: applicable installed skills, official starters and creator material; official documentation and repositories; then stable community solutions.
2. For every viable option record author or organization, official status, version or commit, checked date, material age, stable release, runtime compatibility, project activity and maintenance status, commercial-use license, known vulnerabilities, archive or deprecation state, installer-script risk and the change scope of any found skill.
3. Prefer material from the last 12 months; accept up to 24 months when supported and compatible, and older sources only for officially recognized stable standards. Reject alpha, beta, RC, experimental, abandoned, vulnerable or incompatible options.
4. Return at most three options and one recommendation, which may retain the current solution. Share findings in the current session; do not persist a full research report in the target project.

Completion criterion: the recommendation is stable, dated, source-backed and assessed against every listed safety, maintenance, licensing and compatibility property.
